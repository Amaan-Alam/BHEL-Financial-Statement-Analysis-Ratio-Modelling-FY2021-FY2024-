# BHEL Financial Analysis & Dashboard

## Executive Summary

This project presents a comprehensive financial analysis of Bharat Heavy Electricals Limited (BHEL) for FY2021–FY2024 using audited consolidated financial statements prepared under Ind AS.

The analysis evaluates:

- Financial performance trends
- Profitability recovery trajectory
- Liquidity and solvency position
- Working capital efficiency
- Cash flow sustainability
- Leverage and return metrics

The project combines detailed financial modelling with an interactive Excel dashboard to convert raw financial data into strategic business insights and investment-oriented analysis.

# Key Output

## *Revenue Performance*
- Revenue CAGR of **11.3%** from FY21–FY24
- FY24 growth slowed sharply to **2.3%**

## *Profitability Recovery*
- Transition from FY21 net loss to positive PAT
- EBITDA margin remained range-bound near **5%**

## *Cash Flow Deterioration*
- FY24 Operating Cash Flow declined to **₹(3,712.9 Cr)**
- Free Cash Flow turned deeply negative

## *Leverage Expansion*
- Debt increased significantly during FY24
- Debt/EBITDA expanded to **7.24x**
- Interest Coverage weakened to **1.33x**

## *Working Capital Stress*
- DSO increased substantially
- Cash Conversion Cycle deteriorated sharply in FY24

# Analysis

## *Profitability Analysis*
The company demonstrated operational recovery after FY21 losses; however, profitability remains structurally weak due to:

- Low operating leverage
- Margin compression
- Rising finance costs
- High employee cost base

## *Liquidity Analysis*
Although current and quick ratios remained above 1x, underlying liquidity quality weakened because:

- Cash ratio declined steadily
- Liquidity became increasingly dependent on receivables and contract assets
- Operating cash flows turned negative

## *Solvency & Leverage Analysis*
FY24 marked a significant deterioration in financial risk profile:

- Net cash position shifted to net debt
- Debt dependency increased sharply
- Interest servicing capacity weakened materially

## *Working Capital Analysis*
The major financial pressure point identified was working capital absorption:

- Receivables build-up
- Contract asset expansion
- Longer cash conversion cycle
- Borrowing-led liquidity support

## *Strategic Interpretation*
The project concludes that BHEL’s long-term sustainability depends heavily on:

- Working capital normalisation
- Improvement in free cash flow generation
- Revenue acceleration
- Margin improvement through operational efficiency
